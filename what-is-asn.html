An Advanced Shipping Notice (ASN) is a critical electronic document that provides detailed information about a pending delivery to our warehouses before the shipment actually arrives.

Why Is It Important?

The ASN acts as a "virtual packing list" that allows our warehouse management systems (WM9/SCE11) to prepare for your arrival. Without a correct ASN, our teams cannot verify inventory, which leads to significant delays in unloading and payment processing.

Pre-notification: Tells us exactly what items are coming and when.
Efficiency: Allows for rapid "scan-to-verify" receiving.
Accuracy: Prevents discrepancies between what was ordered and what was shipped.
Tracking: Links the physical shipment to the Purchase Order (PO) and Log ID.

Key Components Of An ASN

A complete ASN must include:

  • PO Reference The original Purchase Order number being fulfilled.
  • Itemized SKUs A breakdown of every product and the exact quantity shipped.
  • Logistics Metadata The Log ID (critical for tracking), Invoice Number, and Date.
  • Packaging Specs The total number of Cartons (UDF4) and Pallets (UDF5).
Pro Tip: Always double-check your Log ID. This is the primary reference used by our inbound teams to locate and process your shipment upon arrival.
